1. Data travels through the internet by many ways but one way
is sent in bits by electricity. The rate at which information
is sent by electricity is determined by bandwidth which is the
max capacity and the actual rate is called bit rate.

2. An ip address (internet protocol address) is an address that is unique to
the device, something similar to a street address of a house. we use IPv6
which uses up to 128 bits and can account for 340 undecillion unique
addresses. Your computer uses DNS to look up domain names and get their
ip addresses and connect to that domain.

3.Cookies are data that websites use to remember who you are. This is saved
in your web browser that allows websites to remember you.
